## Scope

<= $36 M deployed capex, 36 months from grant award to commissioning. The reference is a 2 MW continuous-load district, ~5,000 residents equivalent. Three site variants are compared in parallel until a host community is selected.

## Three site variants

| Variant | Primary generation | Backup / storage | When to choose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Inland (default) | Solar PV 8 MWp | PEM electrolyzer 3 MW + 12 t H2 + 1.5 MW FC + 1.5 MWh Li-ion | No water flow on site; high solar resource |
| River-adjacent | Micro-hydro 1.5 MW + Solar PV 3 MWp | Alkaline electrolyzer 1.5 MW + 6 t H2 + 1 MW FC + 1 MWh Li-ion | Year-round flow >= 0.5 m^3/s with usable head |
| Coastal | Tidal stream 1 MW + Solar PV 4 MWp | PEM electrolyzer 2 MW + 8 t H2 + 1 MW FC + 1.5 MWh Li-ion | Tidal range >= 4 m or strong tidal stream |

## Headline numbers (from `model/`)

Generated by `python -m electicity_model.cli render-all`. See `report/_generated_tables.md` for the live table. Indicative values use the private-equity discount rate, with stack-replacement reserves, and IRA section 45V applied to the US inland scenario.

- Inland (US, with 45V): ~$27 M capex, LCOE ~$214/MWh, LCOH ~$1.90/kg.
- River-adjacent (EU, no 45V): ~$20 M capex, LCOE ~$160/MWh, LCOH ~$4.30/kg.
- Coastal (EU, no 45V, tidal pre-commercial): ~$24 M capex, LCOE ~$198/MWh, LCOH ~$5.22/kg.

All three fit the $36 M envelope. River-adjacent wins on LCOE because micro-hydro carries the highest capacity factor of the primary generators. Coastal carries the largest cost-overrun risk because tidal is pre-commercial (TRL 6-7 deployed).

## Site selection criteria

A host site is selected against a deterministic rubric that cross-references the regional scoring model in chapter 10. Each candidate site is evaluated on the dimensions below before the variant choice is committed.

- Resource quality. Solar GHI >= 1,600 kWh/m^2/year for inland; sustained river flow >= 0.5 m^3/s with >= 4 m head for river-adjacent; tidal range >= 4 m or stream >= 2 m/s for coastal. Cross-reference: chapter 10 resource layer.
- Community fit. Population equivalent in the 3,000-8,000 band, with willing host council and a documented offtake agreement for >= 70 % of nameplate. Cross-reference: chapter 10 community-readiness score.
- Grid context. Interconnection queue under 24 months, or islanded operation legally permitted under local code. Cross-reference: chapter 10 grid-access score.
- Water stress. WRI Aqueduct 4.0 baseline below "Extremely High". Sites with higher stress are admitted only with an approved reuse or dry-cooling plan. Cross-reference: chapter 10 water-risk layer.
- Permitting envelope. NFPA 2, ATEX, or AS/NZS 1596 path with documented precedent for hydrogen storage at >= 5 t scale. Cross-reference: chapter 10 regulatory-readiness score.
- Insurance bondability. Specialist energy-infrastructure broker willing to underwrite at the proposed scale. Cross-reference: chapter 07 risk register.

The rubric returns a composite score per site. Sites in the top quartile of the chapter 10 region scoring proceed to detailed engineering. Ties resolve toward the variant with the lower modelled LCOE.

## Why this beats batteries-only at this scale

- A 2 MW district at 17.5 GWh/year with seasonal solar variability needs multi-day to multi-week storage in winter. Li-ion sized for 5 days at 2 MW is 240 MWh. At $320/kWh installed that is ~$77 M, double the entire envelope.
- H2 storage at $450/kg buffer cost places 12 tonnes (~400 MWh chemical) on site for $5.4 M. The round-trip efficiency penalty (~30 %) is the price paid; the model accounts for it through oversized PV.
- The Li-ion buffer carries diurnal cycling (cheaper per cycle than H2). H2 carries multi-day and seasonal balancing. NREL ATB and IEA GHR identify this hybrid as the lowest-LCOE architecture for 2-10 MW community grids without grid backup [NREL-ATB-2024, IEA-GHR-2024].

## Hydrogen safety case (district)

NFPA 2 (US), ATEX zoning per Directive 2014/34/EU and EN 60079 (EU), AS/NZS 1596 and AS 4564 analogues (AU/NZ) [NFPA-2, ATEX-2014-34, AS-NZS-1596]. Concrete commitments:

- Storage in a detached, vented enclosure with >= 5 m setback from occupied buildings.
- Continuous H2 leak detection with alarm at 0.4 vol % (10 % LFL); shutdown at 1 vol %.
- Hydrogen-compatible piping steels (316L stainless or polymer-lined), tested against embrittlement per ASTM G142.
- Annual integrity testing; pressure-relief devices vented to a safe height.
- Insurance through a specialist energy-infrastructure broker (e.g. Marsh McLennan energy practice, Munich Re ERGO HiTech). Bondability is a known bottleneck and is named in the chapter 07 risk register.

## Water draw

At 2 MW continuous load, with H2 delivering ~50 % of energy via the fuel-cell loop, the district consumes ~150 t/year of H2, which corresponds to ~3,000 m^3/year process water at 22 L/kg. Cooling water adds ~5,000 m^3. Total ~8,000 m^3/year. Mapped against WRI Aqueduct 4.0 the project commits to:

- < 0.1 % of basin annual renewal at any host site.
- No host site selected with WRI Aqueduct "Extremely High" baseline water stress without an approved reuse or dry-cooling alternative.

## KPI commitments at end of programme

- Availability >= 99.5 % over 12 months instrumented operation.
- LCOE within +/- 15 % of pre-deployment model.
- Lifecycle GHG < 0.45 kg CO2e per kg H2 (45V Tier 1).
- H2 leakage < 1 % across the value chain.
- Annual water draw < 0.1 % of basin renewal.
- Public dataset of 12 months of operational metrics (Open Science clause).
