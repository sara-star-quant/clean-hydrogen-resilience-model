# 03 - Car Track

> **Disclaimer.** Research document. Not financial, legal, engineering, or tax advice. Not a fundraising solicitation. See [DISCLAIMER.md](../DISCLAIMER.md) at the repository root.

## Scope

<= $1.0 M, 24 months, bench-validated FCEV powertrain for a Class 8 heavy-duty truck (or 12 m bus). The deliverable is a credible techno-economic case that unlocks a follow-on demonstration round (typical $5-15 M). On-road homologation is out of scope.

## Hypothesis

At a delivered green-H2 price of <= $4/kg, an FCEV Class 8 powertrain achieves TCO within 5 % of diesel and within 25 % of BEV across long-haul, back-to-base, cold-climate duty cycles, while delivering refuel times under 15 minutes that BEV depot architectures cannot match without 1 MW+ chargers. The hypothesis fails if measured stack durability falls below 15,000 hours under accelerated stress testing, if bench-measured H2 leakage exceeds 1 %, or if delivered H2 prices in the target 45V/Headstart corridors stay above $6/kg through the validation window.

## Result vs comparators

Fuel and TCO numbers come from `model/`, regenerated by `python -m electicity_model.cli car --scenario <name>`. See [report/_generated_tables.md](_generated_tables.md) for the live table. Headline values:

- FCEV at green-H2 wholesale ($4/kg): ~$0.91/km TCO, fuel ~$0.35/km.
- FCEV at unsubsidised H2 ($6/kg): ~$1.08/km TCO.
- FCEV at premium retail H2 ($9/kg): ~$1.34/km TCO. Uncompetitive with diesel.
- BEV reference: ~$0.71/km TCO at $110/MWh charging. Beats FCEV today on cost.
- Diesel reference: ~$0.78/km TCO at $1.05/L. Beats FCEV at H2 >= $6/kg.

## Honest positioning

BEVs win passenger cars and most regional HD applications today on $/km. The defensible FCEV niche is narrower:

1. Long-haul, continuous-duty, high-payload routes where battery mass is a payload tax.
2. Back-to-base operations with fast-fill (5-15 min) requirements that batteries cannot match without 1 MW+ chargers and grid upgrades that do not yet exist at most depots.
3. Cold-climate routes where battery range derate is severe.
4. Corridors where green-H2 subsidies (IRA section 45V, ARENA Hydrogen Headstart, EU Innovation Fund) drop H2 to $1.50-3.00/kg delivered. At those prices FCEV TCO undercuts diesel by 10-20 %.

The project does not claim FCEV beats BEV across the board. It commits to validating the segment where FCEV wins and quantifying when, by H2 price band, daily mileage, and duty-cycle profile.

**Recommendation.** Proceed with FCEV bench validation now. The segment-specific case is real and funded credibly by H2 Headstart, 45V, and Innovation Fund tailwinds. The pitch is a niche play, not a passenger-car revolution.

## KPI commitments at end of programme

- Stack power density measured at >= 1.0 kW/kg.
- Continuous power >= 80 kW at 70 deg C ambient.
- Cold-start to 50 % power < 60 s at 0 deg C.
- Stack durability projection >= 20,000 hr from accelerated stress test.
- Bench-measured H2 leakage < 1 % across the test campaign.
- Defended TCO model validated against in-test fuel consumption.

## Forward work

- Stack durability correlation between accelerated stress testing and 20,000-hour HD road operation; propose a joint test protocol with one OEM partner.
- On-road demonstrator round at $5-15 M, sized for two trucks over 12 months at a host depot.
- Refuelling-station capex and footprint study at depot scale (350 vs 700 bar tradeoff).
- Sensitivity sweep of TCO against duty cycle (regional drayage, port shuttle, intercity bus) at the H2 price bands measured during validation.
- Insurance bondability case for HD FCEV at fleet scale; carry findings into chapter 07 risk register.
