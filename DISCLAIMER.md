## What this project is

A research and modelling exercise. The repository contains feasibility analysis, a Python
cost model, narrative chapters, grant proposal skeletons, an investor memo template, and a
browser sim. It exists to document an approach and let people stress test it with their own
numbers.

## What this project is not

- **Not financial advice.** Numbers in the model and memos are illustrative. Capex, LCOE,
  LCOH, IRR, NPV, payback, and market sizing are scenario outputs derived from public
  sources cited in `report/references.md`. They are not personalised recommendations and
  not a basis for investment, divestment, hedging, or trading decisions.
- **Not legal advice.** References to the IRA, EU CRMA, RFNBO delegated regulations, NFPA
  2, ATEX, AS/NZS standards, FERC Order 2023, and similar statutes describe the regime as
  understood by the author on 2026-05-05. Compliance, licensing, and contracting decisions
  require a qualified lawyer in the relevant jurisdiction.
- **Not engineering certification.** The architectures described here are paper designs.
  None of them have been built or commissioned. Real construction needs site specific
  engineering, permitting, and certification by competent professionals.
- **Not a tax opinion.** Tax credit references including IRA section 45V, EU ETS, and
  state level instruments are simplified for modelling. A tax advisor must size them for a
  specific entity and structure.
- **Not a fundraising solicitation.** This repository is not seeking investment, debt,
  equity, partnership, donations, or commitments. The investor memo is a template that
  illustrates what a memo for a project of this shape might contain. Take it as a worked
  example, not a pitch.
- **Not a security or token offering.** Nothing in the repository is an offer or sale of
  any security, token, derivative, or financial instrument under the laws of any
  jurisdiction.
- **Not safety guidance for hydrogen handling.** Sections referencing leak detection,
  embrittlement, ATEX zoning, and pressure relief are illustrative. Real hydrogen
  facilities require certified safety cases, qualified contractors, and insurance.
