## 1. Scheme summary

Horizon Europe is the Union's R&I framework programme. Cluster 5 (Climate, Energy
and Mobility) is the cluster of relevance to this proposal, with two of its six
destinations directly applicable:

- Destination 2. Cross-sectoral solutions for the climate transition (energy
  supply, including renewable hydrogen production and integration).
- Destination 3. Sustainable, secure and competitive energy supply (efficient,
  sustainable and inclusive energy use; storage; flexibility; system
  integration).

Typical award envelopes in recent Cluster 5 work programmes have ranged from
roughly EUR 5M to EUR 25M for RIA and IA topics, at TRL bands 4 to 7 (RIA leans
4 to 6, IA leans 5 to 7). The funding rate is 100% of eligible direct cost for
RIA and 70% for IA (100% for non-profit), with a 25% flat-rate indirect cost
allowance. The project's two tracks straddle this band: the FCEV bench
campaign sits at TRL 5 to 6 and is RIA-shaped; the 2 MW district build sits at
TRL 6 to 7 and is IA-shaped. Submitting under a single bifurcated topic, or
under two coordinated topics in the same destination cluster, is the recommended
structure.

The strategic policy backdrop is the EU Hydrogen Strategy [EC-H2-Strategy] and
the REPowerEU programme [EC-REPowerEU]. Both name renewable hydrogen as a
priority and identify integration with variable renewables and end-use
hard-to-abate sectors as the key remaining demonstration gap.

## 2. Call fit narrative

The project maps to Cluster 5 Destination 2 (clean energy supply) for the 2 MW
district hybrid microgrid track, which produces RFNBO-compliant renewable
hydrogen from co-located variable renewable input and demonstrates
system-integration with grid ancillary services. It maps to Destination 3
(efficient end-use) for the FCEV Class 8 bench-validation track, which
demonstrates a hard-to-abate freight-transport end-use of that same hydrogen
vector.

The bifurcation matches the typical Cluster 5 split between supply-side and

## 3. Excellence

Reuse [_shared_narrative.md](_shared_narrative.md) Block: Technology approach (full text) for the
plain-language summary of T1 through T7. The Theory of Change applicable to
this proposal is set out in [_shared_narrative.md](_shared_narrative.md) Block: Theory of Change and
is referenced in the Impact pathway in section 4.

Ambition. The project advances the European state of the art on three fronts.
First, RFNBO-compliant variable-input electrolyser operation at MW scale is
demonstrated under measured renewable input rather than under a synthetic
duty profile, closing a durability and degradation evidence gap identified
in [IEA-GHR-2024]. Second, the same hydrogen vector is exercised at vehicle
scale (FCEV bench) and at community scale (2 MW district), producing
peer-comparable measurement campaigns rather than two unrelated demonstrators.
Third, the lifecycle GHG and leakage measurement programme produces public
data at a fidelity not available in the current literature, addressing the
hydrogen-as-warming-agent question raised in [Sand-2023].

Objectives. Deliver the nine quantified KPIs in Block: Quantified KPIs;
publish the engineering and economic model, parameter set, and quarterly
telemetry under Open Science terms; deliver one peer-reviewed dataset paper
and at least two methods papers within 24 months of project end.

Methodology. Sequential gating: the FCEV bench campaign (24 months) closes
before the district build is committed, so that fuel-cell durability and
hydrogen-quality findings inform the district storage and dispense
specification. The district build (36 months) overlaps the second year of
the bench campaign, with measurement campaign (WP5) starting from month 30.

## 4. Impact pathway

The project's impact pathway follows the explicit Theory of Change in
[_shared_narrative.md](_shared_narrative.md) Block: Theory of Change, with intervention,
short-term, medium-term, and long-term outcomes; indicators; and
assumptions. The quantified horizons below restate that block in
proposal-table form.

By year 3 (project end): one operational 2 MW RFNBO-compliant district
demonstrator producing public telemetry; one bench-validated Class 8 FCEV
powertrain dataset; nine measured KPIs (Block: Quantified KPIs) published
under Open Science terms; ten to fifteen full-time equivalent jobs
sustained across the consortium.

By year 5 (post-project): two to four replication sites in the EU (see the
geography list in the Innovation Fund skeleton) producing approximately
50 MW cumulative deployable electrolyser capacity in the same configuration;
one to three commercial FCEV fleet pilots adopting the bench-validated
specification; first measured contribution to the REPowerEU [EC-REPowerEU]
10 Mt domestic renewable hydrogen target.

By year 10: replication-class deployment in EU geographies as listed in the
Innovation Fund skeleton, with a deployable footprint of approximately 0.5
to 1.5 GW of electrolyser capacity at the project's measured RFNBO-
compliant duty cycle, contributing on the order of 0.5 to 2.0 Mt CO2e
avoided per annum at full operating duty (calculated against the diesel and
grid-mix counterfactual; figures indicative and to be reset against the
work programme's expected-impact KPIs at submission).

Quantified outcomes are anchored to KPIs 1 through 9 in Block: Quantified
KPIs and reported quarterly through the open-data platform.

## 5. Implementation. Work Packages

Work-package structure. Costs are indicative and must be reset to
call-specific unit costs at submission.

- WP1. Project management, ethics, RRI, GEP. Lead: coordinator. Months 1-36.
  Indicative cost: $0.8M. Deliverables: DMP, ethics protocol, Gender
  Equality Plan consortium aggregate, quarterly reports.
- WP2. FCEV Class 8 bench campaign. Lead: FCEV OEM partner. Months 1-24.
  Indicative cost: $1.0M (full FCEV envelope from project plan).
  Deliverables: bench-validated powertrain dataset, fuel-cell durability
  report, dispense-to-tank measurement campaign.
- WP3. District design and permitting. Lead: EPC partner. Months 1-12.
  Indicative cost: $2.0M. Deliverables: site permit dossier, RFNBO-
  compliant PPA structure, grid interconnection agreement, decommissioning
  bond.
- WP4. District build and commissioning. Lead: EPC partner. Months 12-30.
  Indicative cost: $24.0M (anchored to the model's CapEx range of $20.3M to
  $27.0M, see live numbers below). Deliverables: commissioned 2 MW district
  hybrid; commissioning leak test gate (R1 mitigation).
- WP5. Measurement campaign. Lead: university / national lab. Months 24-36.
  Indicative cost: $4.0M. Deliverables: nine KPI reports, lifecycle GHG and
  H2 leakage dataset, RFNBO compliance audit.
- WP6. Dissemination and Open Science. Lead: dissemination partner.
  Months 1-36. Indicative cost: $2.0M. Deliverables: open model release,
  parameter file release, quarterly telemetry releases, Open Research
  Europe and Zenodo deposits, peer-reviewed publications.
- WP7. Safety case and standards. Lead: ethics and safety advisor.
  Months 1-36. Indicative cost: $1.2M. Deliverables: safety case (HAZOP
  and HAZID), CEN/CENELEC and ISO TC 197 liaison record, coordinated
  vulnerability disclosure policy and incident-response procedure.

Live numbers from the open model anchor WP4:
- $27.0M for inland PV-driven configuration; $20.3M for micro-hydro
  configuration; $24.2M for tidal-coastal configuration [model-tables].
- LCOH range $1.90/kg (PV) to $5.22/kg (tidal) [model-tables].

## 8. Risks, ethics, Open Science

Risk top 5. Import [_shared_narrative.md](_shared_narrative.md) Block: Risk top 5. R1 (leakage),
R2 (bondability), R4 (interconnect), R6 (RFNBO compliance), R8 (tidal
pre-commercial). Each risk is mapped to a WP lead at submission and to a
dedicated contingency line in the budget.

Ethics, gender equality, and public engagement. Import
[_shared_narrative.md](_shared_narrative.md) Block: Ethics, gender equality plan, and public
engagement. The Gender Equality Plan is a Horizon Europe eligibility
condition for participating beneficiaries; the consortium-level GEP
deliverable is owned by WP1 and aggregates the partner GEPs.

GDPR and data protection. Import [_shared_narrative.md](_shared_narrative.md) Block: GDPR and
data protection. The DPO contact is recorded at grant agreement entry
into force; the joint-controller agreement is signed before any
personal-data processing begins.

Coordinated vulnerability disclosure. Import [_shared_narrative.md](_shared_narrative.md)
Block: Coordinated vulnerability disclosure. The CVD policy is published
at month three, integrated with WP7 safety-case work, and aligned with
NIS2 reporting obligations where the host district is in scope.

Open Science. Import [_shared_narrative.md](_shared_narrative.md) Block: Open Science / data
management baseline. The Data Management Plan is mandatory under Horizon
Europe and is delivered at month six, updated at month eighteen and at
project end, following the Horizon Europe DMP template. Peer-reviewed
publications are deposited in Open Research Europe under the platform's
open-access terms; underlying datasets are deposited in Zenodo with
persistent DOIs and FAIR-compliant metadata. The model code is released
under an OSI-approved licence with parameters, unit tests, and a
versioned changelog so that reviewers and replicators can rerun cited
tables.

RFNBO compliance is treated as a binding constraint, not a deliverable.
The RFNBO production methodology [EU-RFNBO-1184] and the lifecycle GHG
methodology [EU-RFNBO-1185] are encoded in the operating-rule library at
WP3 and verified by a third party at WP5.
