## 1. Executive summary

*Indicative template. Not a fundraising solicitation. Numbers are scenario outputs, not forecasts.*

ELECTICITY is two tracks on one financial spine. The idea is plain. Prove integration economics on the smallest unit a balance sheet can underwrite, then replicate. Track A is a Class 8 FCEV truck bench under **$1M** all-in, built to publish bankable per-km TCO at three H2 price points. Track B is a 2 MW district hybrid microgrid under **$36M**, built to publish bankable LCOE/LCOH together with twelve months of resilience telemetry that a financier will actually accept.

The live model anchors the thesis. Every district scenario fits inside the $36M envelope. Solar+H2 inland prints **$26.9M / $214 MWh / $1.90 kg-H2**. Micro-hydro+river prints **$20.3M / $159 MWh / $4.30 kg-H2**. Tidal coastal prints **$24.2M / $198 MWh / $5.22 kg-H2**. The truck bench at $4/kg green-H2 lands at **$0.907/km** against a diesel reference of **$0.781/km** and a BEV reference of **$0.712/km**. Read that honestly. FCEV competes with diesel only where delivered H2 sits below $4/kg. That is precisely the regime IRA section 45V Tier-1 unlocks in the US, and the regime Hydrogen Headstart targets in Australia.

We do not over-claim. BEV beats FCEV for passenger and most regional duty cycles, and we say so in print. The FCEV thesis is narrowly defensible: heavy-duty long-haul, back-to-base drayage, and refuel-time-constrained fleets. We treat LENR as not investable per [Berlinguette-2019]. The defensible asset is not a cell or a stack. It is the **integration methodology, the safety case, and the twelve-month telemetry record** that these two benches generate.

Why now. The policy stack will not be richer than it is in the 2026-2030 window. IRA section 45V is in force as of this memo [IRA-45V]. EU CRMA Strategic Project designations are being executed against [EC-REPowerEU]. ARENA Hydrogen Headstart AUD 2B is disbursing [ARENA-H2]. AEMO ISP 2024 anchors AU grid-side capex into the same window [AEMO-ISP-2024]. Capital deployed in 2026-2027 captures the full subsidy stack on a ten-year asset. Capital deployed later does not.

## 3. Problem

*Indicative template. Not a fundraising solicitation. Numbers are scenario outputs, not forecasts.*

The energy transition stated in dollars rather than slogans. Global hydrogen demand reached roughly 97 Mt in 2024. Less than 1 Mt of that was low-emissions [IEA-GHR-2025]. So the gap is about 96 Mt, and policy is committed to closing it by 2030. At a conservative IRENA 2030 traded-H2 price near $2-3/kg [IRENA-RPGC-2024], the implied annual market is multi-hundred-billion dollars, built from a near-zero base. That is the headline.

Now the parallel story. AEMO ISP 2024 books around AUD 120B of grid-side capex through 2050. Inside that envelope, 1-10 MW distributed firming is named explicitly as a least-regret category [AEMO-ISP-2024]. The unit being underwritten is a firmed renewable enclave with hydrogen carrying multi-day storage. There is no reference design with public bankable economics for it yet. ELECTICITY produces that reference design.

A second demand pull sits adjacent. Heavy-duty trucking is roughly 7-8% of global transport CO2. It is also the only road segment where BEV economics are still genuinely contested at duty cycles above 500 km regional [DOE-HD-FCEV]. The FCEV opportunity is narrow but real, and right now no one has published a third-party-verifiable bench TCO for it.

## 4. Solution

*Indicative template. Not a fundraising solicitation. Numbers are scenario outputs, not forecasts.*

**Two tracks, one methodology.**

**Track A: Class 8 FCEV truck bench (<$1M).** A stationary powertrain bench replicating a Class 8 drayage duty cycle. Outputs: per-km TCO at $4/$6/$9 H2; thermal management envelope; PGM-loading sensitivity; refuel cycle duration. Live model output: $0.907 / $1.082 / $1.343 per km at $4/$6/$9 H2 respectively, against diesel ref $0.781/km and BEV ref $0.712/km. Read it straight. BEV wins on TCO today. The defensible FCEV niche sits in the duty cycles where battery mass, dwell-time, or back-to-base refuel logistics flip the comparison.

**Track B: 2 MW district hybrid microgrid (<$36M).** A community-scale firmed renewable enclave with electrolyser plus storage. Three site archetypes already modelled inside the $36M envelope (see section 1). Outputs: 12-month measured LCOE, LCOH, capacity factor, resilience hours, leakage rate, carbon intensity certified to RFNBO methodology [EU-RFNBO-1184].

**Defensibility, in three named moats.** First, a 24-month bench data lead. By the time fast-followers commission an equivalent rig, our published telemetry is two product cycles deep and is being cited by financiers underwriting third-party deployments. Second, a regulatory navigation lead. RFNBO Article 3 hourly matching, IRA section 45V Tier-1 LCA boundary work, FERC Order 2023 interconnect queue choreography. None of it is patentable. All of it compounds with reps. Third, a supply-resilience playbook held as IP. PGM dual-source qualification, AEM/PEM stack hedge, electrolyser long-lead procurement timing, all documented as internal trade secret. None of these moats requires a component cost lead.

**Honest read of the competitive set.** The visible peer group includes listed and recently-listed names in the heavy-duty FCEV class and in microgrid integration: distressed, bankrupt, mature, and well-capitalised cases all sit in the set. ELECTICITY does not compete with any of them on component cost. We compete on **published, third-party-verifiable integration economics at the smallest unit a balance sheet can underwrite**. None of the public peers currently occupies that position with public data.

## 5. IP strategy

*Indicative template. Not a fundraising solicitation. Numbers are scenario outputs, not forecasts.*

Three buckets. Each picked for a specific reason.

**Patentable.** The segment-specific bench-TCO methodology. We file process claims around the bench instrumentation chain, the duty-cycle parameterisation, and the LCA boundary toolchain that produces a number a financier will sign on. Patents are filed in the US, EU, AU, JP, and KR. The filing strategy is defensive. Block fast-followers from claiming our methodology. Do not pursue royalty enforcement.

**Trade secret.** Integration know-how. The procurement timing playbook, vendor qualification matrix, commissioning runbook, and incident response procedures live in the data room behind NDA. The safety case for combined hydrogen plus battery plus grid-tied operation is a defensible operating asset. It is documented internally and disclosed in regulatory filings only.

**Open by design.** The cost model and `tech_params.yaml`. We publish these on purpose, for two reasons. One, grant funders reward transparency, and an open model is a citation magnet that compounds policy-side credibility. Two, open parameters narrow the surface fast-followers can attack on, because they cannot claim a methodology lead they did not build.

**Defensive publishing for fast-followers.** Anything we choose not to patent and that we do not need to keep as trade secret, we publish in a timestamped technical note. That blocks competitors from later patenting the same technique. The cost of a technical note is approximately zero. The cost of being sued under a fast-follower patent is not.

## 8. Go-to-market

*Indicative template. Not a fundraising solicitation. Numbers are scenario outputs, not forecasts.*

**Initial customer profile.** Two named archetypes. First, a public-utility or municipal pilot district with a federally-funded resilience mandate, in the 2-5 MW range, that needs published telemetry to satisfy a state PUC. Second, a heavy-duty fleet operator running back-to-base drayage at a port or rail terminal, where refuel-time and dwell-time considerations flip the BEV/FCEV comparison. Both customers buy on third-party-verifiable data. Glossy slides do not move them.

**24-month sales motion.** Months 0-6: pre-FID engagement with three pilot districts and two fleet operators. Months 6-12: site selection, interconnect queue position, vendor LOIs. Months 12-18: design freeze, permits, financial close on Tranche 2 and Tranche 3. Months 18-24: long-lead procurement and civil works begin. Milestones gate the motion, not quarters. We do not push a deal that is not financeable.

**Channel strategy.** Direct for the first five deployments. The reasoning is simple. Integration risk is highest before the reference design has been replicated, and direct delivery captures the safety-case and runbook learning. After deployment five we transition to OEM partnership for the truck bench (likely a tier-1 OEM in the heavy-duty FCEV class, or a tier-1 EPC for the microgrid track) and retain direct delivery for resilience-premium customers where the margin justifies it.

**Reference customer leverage.** The first pilot district is, by design, a publishable case study. Master service agreement Annex C requires the customer to permit anonymised telemetry release at month 12. That is the asset we sell against for deployments two through five. Pricing for deployment one reflects this. The reference customer pays roughly 60-70% of list, in exchange for the publication right.

### Treasury and working capital

24-month runway target. The $2M working-capital line in Sources and Uses is sized to cover operating cash burn through M5, with a 3-month buffer beyond that gate. Cash never runs below 6 months at any point in the plan.

Currency exposure. USD is the primary operating currency. EUR (Horizon Europe), AUD (ARENA Hydrogen Headstart), and NZD (ERP2) appear on the grant side. Draw mechanism. Capital is structured as milestone-tied tranches, not lump-sum. The reasoning is two-sided. ## 14. Returns and exit

*Indicative template. Not a fundraising solicitation. Numbers are scenario outputs, not forecasts.*

### Public peer bands (HD FCEV class)

Public filings of the listed peers in this class show 10-15x EBITDA at scale, with high variance. Outcomes within the class span distressed and bankrupt component-only theses through to well-capitalised strategic OEMs. The read-through for ELECTICITY is consistent across the set: a component-only thesis without a published integration story does not hold, and capital intensity without third-party-verifiable TCO does not survive a downturn.

Public filings of the listed peers in the microgrid-integrator and fuel-cell class show 0.8-1.5x revenue and 12-18x EBITDA at scale, with high variance, in mature outcomes.
